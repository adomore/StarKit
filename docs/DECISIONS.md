- **D-005** 2026-07-16 — ICC transform backend (lcms2 vs qcms vs minimal built-in sRGB/AdobeRGB/ProPhoto curves) intentionally undecided; decide at T1-1 and record here (license check per INV-6).
- **D-006** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **`starkit-fixtures` carries its own serde mirror of catalog schema v1** (`crates/starkit-fixtures/src/catalog.rs`) instead of depending on `starkit-core::types`. Context: FIXTURES.md names `starkit-core/src/types.rs` the Rust source of truth for the schema, but the non-circularity rule forbids the fixture generator from depending on the crate it measures — truth must not be produced by the code under test. Decision confirmed by the product owner before implementation. Alternatives: (a) let fixtures depend on `starkit-core` — rejected, breaks non-circularity; (b) extract a third shared `starkit-schema` crate — rejected, it would re-couple the two sides through one definition, which is exactly what the rule exists to prevent. Cost: the duplication is real and must not silently drift; **T0-3 owes a cross-check test that the two definitions agree**, and both files point at each other.
- **D-007** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **Canonical per-suite seeds are project constants:** `basic-5k` 1000001, `dense-core` 1000002, `saturated` 1000003, `pairs` 1000004, `nightscape-fg` 1000005. They are committed in each suite's `params.json`, pinned by `fixtures/expected/MANIFEST.sha256`, and asserted in `suite::tests::canonical_seeds_are_pinned`. Changing one invalidates every committed hash and every quality number ever measured against that suite, so it requires a new D-entry. The values themselves are arbitrary (distinct, readable); nothing depends on their magnitude. `gen --suite all` refuses a `--seed` override for the same reason.
- **D-008** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **Fixture generator dependencies**, all license-clean per INV-6: `rand` 0.10.2, `rand_chacha` 0.10.0, `rand_distr` 0.6.0, `png` 0.18.1, `sha2` 0.11.0, `clap` 4.6.2, `tempfile` 3 (dev) — all MIT OR Apache-2.0; `tiff` 0.11.3 — MIT; plus workspace `serde`/`serde_json`/`thiserror`/`anyhow` (MIT OR Apache-2.0). The three `rand*` crates are pinned **exactly** (`=x.y.z`): their RNG stream and distribution sampling algorithms *are* the output-byte contract, so a semver-compatible bump could silently invalidate the committed manifest. Bumping them is deliberate: regenerate fixtures and record a D-entry. The others are ordinary caret deps — their output is byte-checked by the AC tests, which would fail loudly rather than quietly.
- **D-009** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **Serialization policy for hash-pinned artifacts.** JSON: `serde_json::to_string_pretty` (2-space indent, LF, `ryu` shortest-round-trip floats — no locale, no platform-dependent `%g`), plus exactly one trailing newline; pinned by `emit::tests::float_json_formatting_is_pinned`. Manifest: `sha256sum` format (`<hex>  <path>`), paths **relative to the output root** with forward slashes, sorted byte-wise — so no absolute path can leak into a committed file and the manifest is identical regardless of where it was generated. A `.gitattributes` marks `fixtures/expected/** -text`: without it, a Windows checkout would rewrite LF→CRLF in the committed JSON and break every hash.
- **D-010** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **Photometric conventions for truth catalogs**, needed because FIXTURES.md schema v1 says only "linear units" and has no colour field. (1) `gain_adu_per_e = 1.0` in every suite, so electrons and ADU are numerically equal. (2) `flux`/`peak` describe the **noiseless, mean-of-RGB-channels** star signal, excluding background; per-star tint is normalised to mean 1.0 across channels, so a consumer measuring `(R+G+B)/3` sees exactly the catalog numbers — this is the contract T0-2's oracle must measure against. (3) `peak` is the **pre-clip** value: a saturated star's true peak is recorded even though the image clips at 65535. (4) Halo and bleed components on the `saturated` suite are *additional* signal and deliberately **not** included in `flux`, which stays the PSF core flux that detection actually measures. Alternative for (4) — folding halo flux into `flux` — was rejected: it would make the catalog disagree with any sane aperture photometry.
- **D-011** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **T0-1 acceptance tests are split by cost.** The default `cargo test` runs byte-identity on tiny ad-hoc params (covering all four artifact types incl. the sky-mask PNG) plus schema/population validation of the *committed* truth catalogs for all five real suites. Regenerating the five real suites (~10⁸ Poisson draws; 5m39s in release, far longer in debug) is `#[ignore]`d and runs via `cargo test --release -- --ignored`, wired into `ci.sh` at T0-4. Approved by the product owner before implementation. Rationale: a default test run that takes minutes stops being run. Both AC tests exist at full scale and were executed on the final code — they are gated, not skipped.
- **D-012** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **Determinism guarantee is same-toolchain/same-platform**, which is what INV-2 ("same input + same params ⇒ bit-identical output") and PRD §6 ("no wall-clock or thread-order dependence") actually require, and what the T0-1 AC tests. Cross-*platform* byte-identity is **not** guaranteed and is not achievable at reasonable cost: `rand_distr`'s Poisson/Normal samplers route transcendentals through the platform libm, so a Linux CI regenerating fixtures could produce different bytes from this Windows-generated manifest even at the same seed. Committed manifest hashes therefore pin *this* platform's output. **Open for T0-4:** either restrict the CI manifest check to the platform that generated it, or commit per-platform manifests, or (expensive) route every transcendental through a bit-exact `libm` and hand-roll the samplers. Flagged rather than decided, since it shapes ci.sh's design.
- **D-013** 2026-07-16 (T0-1) — **The `saturated` suite's bleed column carries an explicit stylization factor** (`halo.bleed_length_gain`, default 6.0). Context: FIXTURES.md asks for a "halo/bleed approximation", and the first implementation derived the column length physically from the star's overflow charge. Measured on the generated fixture, that produced a saturated extent of **5 px vertical × 5 px horizontal** — i.e. a column shorter than the already-saturated core, so `bleed: true` rendered identically to `bleed: false` while the committed params claimed otherwise. The cause is not a coding bug: at this suite's brightnesses (peaks ≈ 4–7 × full well) the physically implied column genuinely is that short — real blooming needs far more overexposure plus well geometry this generator does not model. Decision: keep the overflow-charge derivation, scale it by an explicit, honestly-named factor, and prove the effect with a test (`render::tests::bleed_elongates_the_saturated_core_vertically`, which asserts `bleed:true` elongates vertically vs `bleed:false` and does not widen horizontally). Result on the committed fixture: 27 px vertical × 5 px horizontal. Alternative — set `bleed: false` and drop the code — rejected: FIXTURES.md asks for the bleed, and its purpose is to give Phase-1 mask code saturated-star structure to cope with. This is a parameter default, hence decided rather than escalated; the *model* is unchanged.
- **D-014** 2026-07-16 — **Q5 answered: public release under MIT.** The GitHub repository `adomore/StarKit` is public and carries an MIT `LICENSE`, both created deliberately by the product owner (confirmed 2026-07-16). This closes Q5 and **supersedes D-003**: `license = "MIT"` is now set in `[workspace.package]` and inherited by all four crates. `publish = false` stays — publishing source publicly and releasing to crates.io are separate decisions, and the latter is not on the table. Consequence for INV-6: the permissive-only dependency rule is now load-bearing for redistribution, not merely hygiene; every dependency added from here is checked against MIT compatibility. The Gate G0 checklist item "Q3–Q6 answered or deferred" is one closer; Q3/Q4/Q6 remain open.
- **D-015** 2026-07-16 — **README preview images are committed under `docs/images/`, regenerated by `tools/make_previews.py`.** Context: the repository is now public (D-014) and its subject is images, but the fixture TIFFs are gitignored and 400 MB, so the landing page had nothing to show. Decision: commit two small PNGs (~1.2 MB total) and the script that produces them, so they are reproducible rather than mystery binaries. The script applies an **asinh display stretch** — fixture images are linear 16-bit and render almost black otherwise. That stretch is presentation only: it touches no committed fixture, no hash, and no photometry. `tools/` is documentation tooling, explicitly not product code and not on any test or build path, so INV-5 is unaffected. Alternatives: (a) no images — rejected, poor for a visual project; (b) generate previews in CI — rejected, needs the 400 MB fixtures and a Python stack for a cosmetic artifact; (c) link to externally hosted images — rejected, adds a dependency outside the repo. Known cost: the PNGs can drift if suite params change; they are cosmetic, so the fix is to re-run the script, not to gate it.
- **D-016** 2026-07-16 — **The GitHub landing page is Chinese; the English version moves to `README.en.md`.** Requested by the product owner. This is a deliberate exception to the CLAUDE.md convention "all code, comments, commits, and docs in English", which has been amended in place to record it rather than left to silently contradict the repo. Rationale: the landing page's readers are the product owner and the primary user (a Chinese-speaking photographer); the convention exists to keep the *engineering* artifacts in one language, not to pick the shop window's. It extends the pattern `PRD.md` ⇄ `docs/PRD-zh.md` already established in the scaffold. Cost: the two READMEs can drift — they are hand-maintained mirrors with no test, so a substantive edit to one must be applied to the other in the same commit.
- **D-017** 2026-07-16 (T0-2) — **Truth `snr` is PER-CHANNEL peak SNR; the definition is pinned in FIXTURES.md rather than changed.** Found while calibrating the oracle: T0-1 renders `snr = peak / sqrt(peak + bkg + read²)` using single-channel noise, while D-010 declares the catalog describes the *mean-of-RGB* plane. Averaging three channels cuts noise by exactly √3 (confirmed: predicted mean-plane background RMS 11.02 vs 10.95 measured), so the stored number is not the SNR of the plane a consumer measures. Escalated to the product owner rather than reinterpreted, because it changes what every SNR-conditioned AC means. **Decision: keep per-channel and document it loudly.** The two framings select identical stars — per-channel `snr ≥ 5` ≡ mean-of-RGB `snr ≥ 8.66` — so this is a labelling choice, not a difficulty one; but conditioning FR-2's existing "≥ 5" bar on the mean-of-RGB value makes it *unachievable at any threshold* (measured on basic-5k: recall peaks at 97.19 %, and only at 25.5 % precision). Alternatives: (a) switch to mean-of-RGB and restate the AC as ≥ 8.66 — equivalent but requires regenerating all five suites, the manifest and every committed catalog, and reads like moving the goalposts; (b) switch and keep "≥ 5" — rejected, it invents a bar the reference instrument cannot clear. **Cost, stated plainly:** "recall ≥ 98 % at SNR ≥ 5" is an easier achievement than it sounds, because those stars sit at ≈ 8.7 σ in the measured plane. FIXTURES.md and every emitted report now carry that caveat so the number cannot be quoted without it.
- **D-018** 2026-07-16 (T0-2) — **The oracle measures FWHM by an elliptical Gaussian2D fit, with a characterised bias, not by image moments.** Image second moments were the first implementation and are broken for this fixture family: measured +36 % against truth (and +56 % over the full matched population). Cause is not a coding bug — a Moffat's second moment is dominated by its wings and depends on β, which varies per star in [2.5, 4.5], so moments measure the wings rather than the half-max width. An astropy `Gaussian2D` + `TRFLSQFitter` fit is used instead: **+2.3 % on elliptical-Gaussian stars** (residual pixel-integration bias — the fixture integrates the profile over each pixel, the model is evaluated at pixel centres) and **+14.2 % on circular-Moffat stars** (model mismatch). Both are flat across fit-box sizes, i.e. explainable systematics rather than tuning artifacts. The suites are ~60 % Moffat so the reported figure (~10 %) is dominated by the Moffat term. Deliberately *not* tuned away by fitting Moffat-and-Gaussian and selecting by residual: that would match the instrument to the generator's exact PSF family and weaken the independence the oracle exists for. Acceptable because FWHM is in no T0-2 acceptance criterion, and T1-3's oracle cross-check is **recall only** — starkit-core's own "median FWHM error ≤ 10 %" is graded against *truth*, not against the oracle. Isophotal masking scored better (+4.65 %) but only by accident of the isophote cancelling the wing inflation, and it drifts with brightness; a lucky number was rejected in favour of an explainable one.
- **D-019** 2026-07-16 (T0-2) — **On the nightscape the oracle excludes the silhouette plus a kernel radius from detection.** Untreated, `nightscape-fg` scored **59 % precision** on 4175 false positives, 99.9 % of them within 20 px of foreground (median 2.8 px). The cause is not the background model and not the foreground texture — both were tested and cleared. At one such false positive the background estimate was 300.1, the data 298.3, i.e. a residual of **−1.8** against a threshold of 55, and DAOStarFinder fired anyway. Only the convolved image explains it: DAOFIND correlates a **zero-sum** kernel, so it responds to how much brighter a pixel is than its neighbourhood, and ordinary sky beside a dark tree silhouette wins that comparison outright. Fix: pass a detection mask of the foreground dilated by the DAOFIND kernel radius (`ceil(1.5 × fwhm / 2.355) + 1`, = 3 px at fwhm 3.2), so no kernel footprint can straddle the sky/silhouette edge. Result: **precision 59.06 % → 99.93 %**. Recall falls 95.74 % → 91.63 %, and that cost is fully accounted for: 4.89 % of SNR ≥ 5 truth stars lie inside the exclusion zone, versus the 4.11 pp observed drop. The oracle now declines to measure 3.5 % of the sky rather than reporting 4175 stars that are not there. `measure.py` therefore takes an optional `--sky-mask` beyond the interface in oracle/CLAUDE.md; for a nightscape it is a correctness requirement, not a convenience. **Note for Phase 1:** starkit-core will meet this same edge effect at the sky-mask boundary (T1-3/T1-5). It is a property of matched-filter detection, not of photutils.
- **D-020** 2026-07-16 (T0-2) — **Precision counts a detection on *any* real star as a success, not only one above the SNR floor.** FIXTURES.md says "recall and precision are computed over truth stars with peak SNR ≥ 5", which is well-defined for recall but ambiguous for precision, since precision is a property of detections. Reading it strictly — only SNR ≥ 5 matches count as true positives — would score a detector *worse* for correctly finding a real SNR 4 star, which inverts the metric's purpose; on basic-5k it reads 82.07 % against 99.87 % for the standard definition. Decision: `precision` = detections matched to any truth star / all detections. The strict variant is emitted alongside as `precision_strict` in every report so the choice is visible and reversible rather than baked into a single number, and both definitions ship in the report's `metric_definitions` block. Decided rather than escalated because the alternative is self-evidently broken; flagged here so it can be overturned cheaply.
- **D-021** 2026-07-16 (T0-2) — **Canonical oracle commands adapted for the venv layout; `pytest` added to `requirements.txt`.** `oracle/CLAUDE.md` and the root `CLAUDE.md` specified `oracle/.venv/bin/pip`, which does not exist on Windows — the venv puts the interpreter at `oracle/.venv/Scripts/python.exe`. The commands now invoke `python -m pip` / `python -m pytest` rather than the `pip` shim (works identically under both layouts) and carry an explicit note about the Windows path. `pytest>=8` joins `requirements.txt` under the existing loose-pin rule, since T0-2's acceptance criteria are encoded as tests and a test runner that is not installed by the documented setup is not a real gate. This is a workflow adaptation, not a spec change: the `measure.py` / `compare.py` interface contract in `oracle/CLAUDE.md` is untouched apart from the `--sky-mask` addition recorded in D-019. T0-4's `ci.sh` will have to pick the right interpreter path per platform; noted there rather than solved here.
- **D-022** 2026-07-16 (T0-3) — **`serde_json` needs `float_roundtrip` + `preserve_order` for the catalog contract to hold.** Found by the T0-3 round-trip test, which failed on the very first committed catalog. Two independent causes, both invisible without a byte-level check. (1) **serde_json's default float parser is not correctly rounded.** `from_str::<f64>("93.07565673947249")` returns bits `…7067`; Rust's own `str::parse` (correctly rounded since 1.55) returns `…7068`. The serializer is fine — ryu is exact — so values written by `starkit-fixtures` came back **1 ULP low on 3702 of 65050 committed floats (~5.7 %)**. Enabling the `float_roundtrip` feature makes the parser exact. This silently invalidated D-009's round-trip claim: the JSON *was* shortest-round-trip on write, but could not be read back. (2) **`serde_json::Value` maps are `BTreeMap` by default**, so the opaque `generator` block re-serialized with keys alphabetised (`background, foreground, halo…` instead of `schema, suite, seed…`). `preserve_order` (via `indexmap`) fixes it. With both: **0 of 65050 lines differ**. New transitive deps `indexmap` 2.14, `hashbrown` 0.17, `equivalent` 1.0 — all `Apache-2.0 OR MIT`, INV-6 clean. **Consequence beyond T0-3:** any Phase-1 code that reads a catalog and writes it back (T1-8's "re-run produces hash-identical outputs") would have corrupted floats without this. Cost: `preserve_order` changes `Value` map behaviour workspace-wide, which is the intent, and `float_roundtrip` trades a little parse speed for correctness — an easy trade for a project whose second invariant is determinism.
- **D-023** 2026-07-16 (T0-3) — **Schema v1 gains an optional `measurement` block; this is the only change from the draft.** The oracle already emitted it (T0-2) but the draft schema had no such field, so `starkit-core` parsed measured catalogs and *silently dropped* their provenance. Approved by the product owner before the freeze. It is the mirror image of `generator`: a truth catalog says how it was generated (params + seed), a measured catalog says how it was measured (method, thresholds, exact library versions), and both are opaque JSON to `starkit-core`, which must not grow an opinion about either. A catalog carries one or the other, never both. Additive and optional, so it breaks no existing reader. Alternative — strip the block from the oracle's catalogs and keep provenance only in the reports — was rejected: `compare.py` already consumes it, and a catalog that outlives its report would then be unable to say what produced it, which is exactly the traceability oracle/CLAUDE.md demands. Phase 1's `starkit-core::detect` gets the same field for free. Everything else in the draft is frozen unchanged; `snr` stays required-in-truth/optional-in-measured, `tier` stays required (the oracle assigns tertiles purely to be schema-valid — evaluation matches by position, never by tier).
- **D-024** 2026-07-16 (T0-4) — **`ci.sh` fails loudly on a manifest mismatch rather than skipping the check off-platform; the cross-platform question (D-012) stays open until a second platform actually exists.** D-012 left T0-4 to choose between restricting the manifest check to its generating host, committing per-platform manifests, or routing every transcendental through a bit-exact libm. Decision: **none of them yet.** The smoke check runs unconditionally on every host; when it fails it prints the host and points at D-012, so a cross-platform difference is diagnosable rather than mysterious. Rationale: per-platform manifests and a hand-rolled libm are both real costs paid to fix a problem no one has yet observed — there is no Linux or macOS CI, and no second machine. Committing to a mechanism now would be guessing at the shape of a failure we cannot reproduce. A silent platform-conditional skip was rejected outright: it would turn "the fixtures are deterministic" into a claim CI stops checking precisely where it is most likely to be false. **Revisit when the first non-Windows host runs `ci.sh`** — that run's diff is the evidence needed to choose. Two-run byte-identity (the actual INV-2 requirement) is platform-independent and is covered on every host by the `--ignored` tests via `./ci.sh --full`.
- **D-025** 2026-07-16 (T0-4) — **`ci.sh` treats a missing oracle venv as a failure, not a skip.** The oracle tests carry T0-2's acceptance criteria, so a run that quietly omits them and prints "all checks passed" is a false green — the single most expensive kind of bug in a test harness. `ci.sh` locates the interpreter itself (`oracle/.venv/bin/python` or `oracle/.venv/Scripts/python.exe`, per D-021) and, if neither exists, fails with the exact setup commands. Auto-creating the venv was rejected: a CI script that installs packages behind the user's back is a surprise, and the failure message costs one command to resolve. `--quick` may skip the fixture smoke (the only step needing a release build) and says so on the line where the check would have appeared; nothing can skip silently.
- **D-026** 2026-07-16 (G0) — **Q1 answered: Windows, standalone GUI.** The photographer is on Windows and the target form factor is a standalone desktop GUI — not a Photoshop plugin. Consequences: Windows 10+ is the primary platform (macOS 13+ stays a stated PRD target but is now untested and unowned — nobody has a Mac on this project); Phase 2 builds `starkit-gui` as planned, and the egui-vs-Tauri decision at G1 exit is live, judged on proxy-preview latency ≤ 200 ms on a 4K proxy, packaging weight and CJK rendering. The PS plugin bridge stays where PRD §9 put it — Phase 3, behind its own mini-PRD — rather than being promoted. It also makes D-012 sharper: the committed fixture manifest was generated on the one platform that actually matters, so the cross-platform question (D-024) is now lower stakes than it looked.
- **D-027** 2026-07-16 (G0) — **Q2 deferred: no real corpus yet; Phase 1 proceeds on synthetic fixtures alone. This defers a debt, it does not cancel one.** The ≥ 20 real 16-bit TIFFs are not available, so G0 closes on the explicit deferral the ROADMAP checklist allows. What that buys: T1-1 … T1-9 are all gradeable against the golden fixtures and the oracle, so Phase 1 development is genuinely unblocked. What it costs, stated plainly rather than discovered later: **gate G1 cannot close.** Specifically — **FR-2's real-corpus acceptance** (visual-QA checklist, oracle cross-check on real images) has nothing to run on; **T1-10 Real-corpus QA round** cannot start; **FR-4's photographer sign-off** ("exported masks usable as-is in her PS workflow on ≥ 5 of her own images") is the literal G1 exit criterion and is unobtainable; and the PRD §11 risk "nebulosity mistaken for stars", whose stated mitigation is validation against the real corpus before G1, stays unmitigated. Synthetic fixtures prove an algorithm is *correct*; only her images prove it is *useful*, and the two are not substitutes — a detector can hit 98 % recall on `basic-5k` and still be worthless on a real Milky Way core. **Revisit before T1-10, and treat the corpus as a G1 blocker, not a nice-to-have.**
- **D-028** 2026-07-16 (G0) — **Q3, Q4 and Q6 deferred to the phase that actually needs them.** None blocks Phase 1. **Q3** (is a full starless layer an artistic deliverable or an internal step?) sets FR-7 polish priority — needed at Phase 2, when FR-7 is built; the reduction path produces it at r = 0 either way. **Q4** (enhancement taste: diffraction spikes and heavy glow, or restrained?) anchors FR-6's default parameters — needed at Phase 2, and best answered with one or two of her own reference "after" images rather than in the abstract. **Q6** (discrete GPU?) only affects Phase 3 wgpu planning. Deferring is not the same as forgetting: each is a named input to a named task, and asking then — with the tool in her hands and something concrete to react to — will get a better answer than asking now. This closes the G0 checklist item "Q3–Q6 answered or deferred with decision notes"; Q5 was already answered in D-014.
